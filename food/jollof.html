Jollof rice for two

Ingredients

  • 1 cup (~190g) long grain easy cook rice

    • For blended sauce
  • 400g tin peeled plum tomatoes
  • Half a bell pepper
  • 1 red onion
  • 3-4 garlic cloves
  • 1 scotch bonnet pepper (without seeds)
  • Thumb sized piece of ginger

    • Additional
  • Quarter cup (59ml) sunflower or vegetable oil
  • 2 tablespoons tomato puree
  • 1 teaspoon paprika powder
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 0.25 teaspoon nutmeg
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 and a half teaspoons salt
  • 1 cup vegetable stock (or just water)

Method

  1. Take 4 peeled tomatoes out of the tin of peeled plum tomatoes (can avoid taking the liquid, or just take a small bit) and blend with half the bell pepper, chopped red onion, garlic cloves, scotch bonnet pepper and ginger until smooth
  2. Heat the oil in a pan big enough for the blended sauce and rice - add the blended sauce to the oil and 1-2 tablespoons of tomato puree - once its sizzling, stir and cover lid, leave for 20 minutes on low heat (check to stir every ~5 minutes)
  3. Rinse rice and assemble seasoning (paprika, thyme, nutmeg, salt) while sauce cooks
  4. Once sauce is cooked, add the rinsed rice, seasoning, bay leaves and stir a little - add the water/stock shortly after such that there is enough to cover the rice, but don't overdo it - make sure rice is sizzling, then leave on low heat, cover with tinfoil and a lid for ~20 minutes (check and stir every ~5 minutes)
  5. Close to 20 minutes, the heat can be turned off if the rice is cooked and the pot is becoming too burned - leave for 5 minutes then serve
